The Story

Built on Adversity.
Driven by Curiosity.

"From a small town in India to a FinTech founding team in Dubai. The arc was not accidental. It was built, one deliberate decision at a time."

I grew up in Narwana, India, where my father worked closely with farmers on rural mechanization. I was born with hearing loss and learned early to listen more carefully than most. When I lost my mother at fifteen, what followed was not a pause. It was a redirection. Grief became fuel. Every exam, every opportunity after that was both tribute and proof.

My career started in strategy at Coca-Cola and evolved into product at a FinTech startup in Dubai. That shift was not a pivot. It was curiosity following its natural direction. Strategy tells you what needs to exist. Product forces you to build it, ship it, and iterate. I discovered I needed both, and the tension between them is where I do my best thinking.

What I have noticed across every role: the best outcomes arrive before the meeting starts. I solve problems by first solving the room. Relationships, trust, and knowing when to listen have delivered more value than any framework I have studied. I build teams that feel ownership. I have been told I make difficult conversations feel manageable. That is the compliment I value most.

Outside work, I cook, hike, and travel. I am a self-confessed foodie who does not follow recipes. I treat them as loose suggestions, freestyle from there, and occasionally produce something genuinely brilliant. The occasional disaster stays between me and the kitchen. Hiking keeps me grounded. There is something about being on a trail, away from screens and notifications, that nothing else quite matches. I also went skydiving over Madrid once at 15,000 feet and survived to recommend it. Travel, though, is the real obsession. Across Japan, Singapore, the US, the UK, Europe, Scandinavia, and beyond, what stays with me is less about the places and more about the people. How different cultures approach the same problem differently. How strangers become connections over a shared meal. That curiosity about people never really leaves me.
